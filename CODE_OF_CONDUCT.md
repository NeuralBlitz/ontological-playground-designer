### *Ontological Playground Designer Community Guidelines*

## üåü Our Commitment

The **Ontological Playground Designer** project and community are dedicated to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and inspiring environment for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We believe that diverse perspectives and respectful collaboration are essential for advancing the frontiers of AI and ontological engineering. Our community thrives on the **Axiom of Universal Love ($\phi_{22}$)** and the **Flourishing Objective ($\phi_1$)** ‚Äì principles deeply embedded in the very fabric of our AI-designed worlds. We extend these same principles to our interactions.

## ü§ù Expected Behavior

To foster a positive environment, we expect participants to:

*   **Be Respectful and Inclusive:** Engage in constructive dialogue. Treat everyone with dignity, respect, and empathy. Be considerate of differing viewpoints and experiences.
*   **Show Empathy and Kindness:** Understand that not every interaction is perfect. Offer and gracefully accept constructive criticism. Focus on what is best for the community.
*   **Practice Active Listening:** In discussions, strive to understand others' perspectives before presenting your own.
*   **Focus on the Work:** Contribute to the project's goals by providing thoughtful ideas, code, and feedback.
*   **Use Welcoming Language:** Avoid derogatory, demeaning, or hostile language. Be mindful of how your words may affect others.
*   **Report Issues Responsibly:** If you observe or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it following the guidelines below.

## üö´ Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable within our community:

*   **Violent Threats or Language:** Direct or indirect threats of violence or hate speech.
*   **Discriminatory Jokes and Language:** Any jokes or language that demeans individuals based on their protected characteristics.
*   **Trolling, Insulting, or Derogatory Comments:** Personal attacks, insults, or put-downs.
*   **Public or Private Harassment:** Intimidation, stalking, or unwanted attention.
*   **Publishing Private Information:** Posting or threatening to post others' personally identifying information ("doxing") without explicit permission.
*   **Unwelcome Sexual Attention:** Inappropriate physical contact, sexualized comments, or advances.
*   **Sustained Disruption of Discussion:** Repeatedly interrupting or derailing conversations.
*   **Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors.**

## üö® Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it as soon as possible. All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

**To report an incident:**

1.  **Contact:** Please reach out directly to nuralnexus@icloud.com  or a designated project maintainer.
2.  **Provide Details:** Include your name (or remain anonymous), the date and time of the incident, a description of the behavior, who was involved, and any relevant context (e.g., chat logs, screenshots).
3.  **Expect a Response:** We will acknowledge your report promptly and outline the next steps for investigation and resolution.

## üë• Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior deemed unacceptable.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
